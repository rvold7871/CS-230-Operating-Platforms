# CS-230-Operating-Platforms

**Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements. Who was the client? What type of software did they want you to design?**  
The Gaming Room, the creator of the game "Draw It or Lose It," is a game development client with limited experience in web-based software development. They aim to expand their user base by extending their game to additional platforms through a web-based application designed for a distributed environment. The application must support the management of one or more teams, each with one or more players. Team and game names must be unique, and users should have the ability to verify the availability of a name before selecting a team name. Additionally, only one instance of a game should be active in memory at any given time.
		
**What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?**  
I thoroughly evaluated various operating platform options, carefully considering their advantages and disadvantages, and provided a well-informed recommendation for the best course of action. Additionally, I effectively communicated my reasoning to the client in a clear and professional manner, ensuring transparency and fostering trust to support moving forward with the proposed approach.

**What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?**  
Expressing the UML diagram in verbal form significantly enhanced my understanding of the overall program structure. While a visual representation of the classes is valuable, describing the interactions between classes in words further solidified my comprehension of the system's architecture. I also found that the process of differentiating among the three primary operating platforms—Mac, Windows, and Linux—was highly beneficial in maintaining a comprehensive understanding of the project's requirements. By analyzing and comparing the strengths and weaknesses of each platform, I was able to efficiently determine the most appropriate choice for server hosting. Additionally, this approach provided insight into the rationale behind each selection, ultimately informing the architectural design of various components within the application.

**If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?**  
I would recommend revising the recommendations section to provide more specific implementation details — for example, suggesting Amazon S3 for storage or PostgreSQL for database operations. I would also include supporting rationale for each choice, such as noting that Linux is a preferred deployment environment for PostgreSQL due to its stability, performance, and alignment with the open-source ecosystem. Finally, I would elaborate on file storage methods, such as Linux's capability to implement a direct storage RAID array, a globally shared file system, and a network file system.

**How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?**  
As a consultant, it is crucial to remain within the scope of the client's defined needs, constraints, and requirements. By taking a holistic approach to the requirements provided by The Gaming Room, I was able to apply object-oriented principles in conjunction with a client-server architecture to satisfy them as a whole, delivering the product the client expected to receive. Considering user needs is essential because it keeps development aligned with the agreed scope, reducing the risk of costly revisions later. Ultimately, a technically sound product that fails to meet user expectations will not be adopted — making user-centered design critical to the success of any software project.

**How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?**  
In developing this software, I employed a top-down approach, starting with a comprehensive analysis of the project requirements and constraints. I then divided the development process into logical components, including architecture, security, storage, and platform compatibility. Once the requirements were clearly understood, developing and interpreting UML diagrams for the program's classes was essential, as they served as a blueprint for the coding process. Additionally, evaluating and comparing various technologies to fulfill the requirements enabled me to select the most appropriate tools for each specific need.
